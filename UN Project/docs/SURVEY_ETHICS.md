# Survey ethics and consent — draft for review

The survey will collect self-reported information from members of the public. Because the project is earmarked for UN handover, we should meet a standard at least as strict as what a university research ethics board would require, even if no formal review is mandated.

## Principles
1. **Informed consent before any data is collected.** Respondent must actively agree.
2. **No personal identifiers collected by default** (no names, no emails, no IP addresses stored with responses).
3. **Respondents can withdraw** their data up to 30 days after submission by contacting a project email.
4. **Data minimisation** — we only ask what we actually plan to use.
5. **Survey responses are stored and published separately from official statistics** and always labeled as community-sourced.
6. **The survey is optional** and anonymous. Nothing is gated behind it.

---

## Suggested consent language (to appear on the first page of the Google Form)

> **Before you begin**
>
> This survey is part of an independent research project being prepared for handover to the United Nations. Its goal is to better understand how people around the world consume media — which platforms they use, what kind of content they look for, and how much they trust different sources.
>
> **The survey is anonymous.** We do not ask for your name, email address, or any other information that could identify you.
>
> **It is voluntary.** You may skip any question, close the page at any point, or decline to participate.
>
> **Your answers will be published in aggregate** — as grouped statistics on our public website — not as individual responses.
>
> **Your data will be stored separately from official statistics** on our map and will always be labeled as community-sourced.
>
> You can request removal of your response (for example, if you change your mind) within 30 days of submission by emailing **[project email to be added]** with the approximate date and country of your submission.
>
> The survey takes about 4 minutes.
>
> - [ ] I have read the information above and I agree to participate.

Without the consent box ticked, the form should not advance.

## Core questions (draft)
Keeping it short. Every question non-required except age band, country, and consent.

1. Consent (required, as above).
2. Which country do you live in? (required, dropdown of all 195 UN member states)
3. What age range are you in? (required: under 18 / 18–24 / 25–34 / 35–44 / 45–54 / 55–64 / 65+)
   - *If under 18: the form should thank them and not proceed.* Minors require a different consent process.
4. Which medium do you use most for news and current events? (TV / Radio / Online news site / Social media / Messaging app / Printed newspaper / Podcast / I don't follow news)
5. On an average day, how many hours do you spend on each of the following? (grid: TV, radio, social media, reading/print, streaming video, podcasts — buckets: 0 / under 1 / 1–2 / 2–4 / 4+)
6. Which topics interest you most? (multi-select: Politics / International news / Local news / Climate & environment / Health / Business / Science & tech / Arts & culture / Sports / Lifestyle)
7. How much do you trust the news you consume? (scale 1–5)
8. How much do you trust each medium? (grid: TV / radio / print / online news sites / social media — scale 1–5)
9. Optional demographics (gender, education, urban/rural) — clearly marked optional.

## Language coverage
Launch in **English** first. Translations (French, Spanish, Arabic, Chinese, Russian — the six UN languages) are a Phase 3 stretch goal. Google Forms supports multiple forms sharing one backend; we'd run parallel forms per language.

## Under-18 policy
We will not knowingly collect responses from minors. The form filters them out at question 3. We accept that this somewhat limits representativeness; the trade-off is clearly justified.

## Data retention
- Raw responses kept in a private Google Sheet accessible only to project maintainers.
- Aggregate (grouped) statistics published on the public site.
- Individual responses never published.
- If the project ends, raw data is deleted within 6 months of handover unless the UN formally takes ownership.
